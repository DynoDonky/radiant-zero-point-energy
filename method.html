Method

Once you are very clear on differentiating substantial and insubstantial you can then beging with one division of yin and yang, you can then move on to another division. Slowly you build more and more layers. You must trust this way, if you have doubts in your mind about Tai Chi it will be difficult to commit your self to the degree required to develop it sufficiently.

One of the interesting analogies knowledgeable Tai Chi people use to explained the methodology is; "Tai Chi is like a computer, it's 1's and 0's. See my hand, this side is yin, the other side is yang. You need to translate this understanding into you'r body, you need to understand how is a 1, how is a 0". This may give you a deeper insight into the absolute nature of the Tai Chi practice and methodology. But still, it's no substitute for the guidance of a true Master. When you really get to train with someone like that, you will start to feel much more inclined to commit more of your time to Tai Chi. If you give some of your time to Tai Chi each day, Tai Chi will give a lot back to you

Tai Chi can be frustrating in the begining. It is a highly tedious, intricate, subtle methodology so delicate and precise, it is difficult to analyse or describe other than going back to it's core principles, which are so devinely simplex, there doesn't seem to be much to go by initially. Be patient, train when you have the time, as you calm your mind and deepen your awareness, over time you'll see more and more.


Class Structure

      1 Mingling

    Some time to chit chat and become aquainted

      2 Attention

    Instructor calls attention, students prepare

      3 Walking

    Students follow instructor in walking excercise

      4 Line-up

    We all line-up and prepare for the class

      5 Bow & Salute

    We bow and salute

      6 Centering

    Centering awareness in natural stance

      7 Standing

    A few minutes in each basic posture

      8 Loosening

    Loosening exercises

      9 Body Alignment

    -Low stance against the wall

    -Nose, knees and toes

    -Heels, back and head

    -Walking with feet parallel

      10 Postures

    Partner exercise. Observe and correct each other

      11 Tai Chi form

    Whole class practices together, then we split into groups and work in groups.

      12 Push-hands preparation exercise

    Loosening exercises preparation for push-hands drills




      13 Partner push-hands drills


      14 Free push-hands

-----------------------------------Finish Class-----------------------------------

Extra Training

The Extra Training is an optional after-class session for those who would like to gradually add a few more layers to the base level to learn to apply Tai Chi under greater levels of pressure by throwing in a few more variables. Step by step, safety first.
